I sit at a rare intersection: a practicing Electronics Controls Engineer at Cummins with hands-on experience designing and validating embedded software for safety-critical engine systems, and a graduate student deepening that foundation with an M.S. in Artificial Intelligence at Indiana Wesleyan University. Most AI practitioners never see their algorithms deployed in regulated engineering environments. Most controls engineers understand the systems but lack the tools to make them intelligent. I bridge both worlds, building AI solutions that are technically rigorous, practically deployable, and trustworthy in the high-stakes contexts where those qualities matter most.

What I Bring
  • Domain-Grounded AI: I apply machine learning to problems I understand at the system level -- engine diagnostics, control optimization, sensor-driven prediction -- not just as academic exercises, but as real engineering solutions.
  • Human-Centered Design Thinking: My AI Lab project demonstrated the full Stanford Design Thinking lifecycle, from empathizing with a non-expert user to deploying a tested, guardrailed conversational AI tool.
  • Principled Algorithm Selection: I understand not just how ML and deep learning work, but when each is the right tool -- a critical competency in regulated industries where interpretability and auditability matter as much as accuracy.
  • Cross-Disciplinary Impact: With a background spanning instrumentation, robotics, control systems, and now AI, I bring a systems-thinking perspective that produces more robust, real-world-ready solutions.
