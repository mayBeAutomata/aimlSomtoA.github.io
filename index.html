What I Bring to the Field

I sit at a rare intersection: a practicing Electronics Controls Engineer at Cummins with hands-on experience designing and validating embedded software for real-world engine systems, now deepening that foundation with graduate-level expertise in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

Most AI practitioners learn the algorithms without ever seeing them deployed in safety-critical, regulated engineering environments. Most controls engineers understand the systems but lack the tools to make them intelligent. I bridge both worlds, which means I can do what neither group can do alone: build AI solutions that are technically rigorous, practically deployable, and trustworthy in high-stakes contexts.

Unique Value
  • Domain-Grounded AI: I apply machine learning to problems I understand at the system level engine diagnostics, control optimization, sensor-driven prediction; not just as academic exercises but as real engineering solutions.
  • Human-Centered Design Thinking: My AI Lab project demonstrated the full Stanford Design Thinking lifecycle, from empathizing with a non-expert user to deploying a tested, guardrailed conversational AI tool.
  • Principled Algorithm Selection: I understand not just how ML and deep learning work, but when each is the right tool such as a critical competency in regulated industries where interpretability and auditability matter as much as accuracy.
  • Cross-Disciplinary Impact: With a background spanning instrumentation, robotics, control systems, and AI, I bring a systems-thinking perspective that produces more robust, real-world-ready solutions.
Who I Serve

Engineering teams and organizations seeking to move beyond rule-based automation toward intelligent, adaptive systems particularly in automotive, industrial, and energy sectors where AI must meet rigorous safety and performance standards.
